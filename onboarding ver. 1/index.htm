First Steps:

  • Complete your Core Beliefs profile and log into the site

    The Core Beliefs Profile is a simple 5-10 minute survey-like questionnaire designed to determine the level of alignment between your views on educational mission and culture and that of the school administration, and speaks to the likelihood that a teacher will be happy at that school long term. myEDmatch will use the results of your Core Beliefs Profile to suggest schools with similar educational missions, beliefs, and goals as your own.

    The questionnaire includes seven core areas of education:

    • Mission - a shared set of beliefs around the purpose, goals, and aspirations of a school
    • Instruction - a specific approach to teaching and learning used in a school
    • Professional Development - support of teachers' professional growth and advancement
    • Planning - organization around the task of teaching and learning
    • Student Culture - students' shared beliefs, values, and behaviors concerning school
    • School Environment - the management of time, people, and resources through systems and structures

    Within each area is a series of statements, about which you will select the degree to which you agree or disagree. To do so, just click on the radio button indicating your level of agreement for each statement. Don't forget to click Save after completing each core section. It should take you no more than 5-10 minutes to complete. Once you've completed this profile, you can begin searching for jobs that are right for you using our more than 20 filters via the "Jobs" tab!

And the best part is, you can build it quickly and easily by following the steps below.

  • Have your Principal Complete the Core Beliefs Profile

    The Core Beliefs Profile provides us a glimpse into your school's mission, culture, and instructional model. Taking no more than 5-10 minutes to complete, we use this profile to determine how good of a "fit" an applicant is for your specific school. To promote retention, Principals are the only individuals at schools who can fill out the Core Beliefs Profile. This is because school leaders are integral to the culture of school, and study after study have shown that misalignment with the mission, vision, and culture set by the school's administration is the primary driver of teacher attrition. Your specific answers to each question will be kept private and will not be shared with potential candidates, but our "Match Score" is calculated on the basis of your answers to these questions.


    Here's how principals complete the Core Beliefs Profile:

    Step 1: When your school account has been activated, your principal will receive an email prompting for password reset. Principals will need to follow the link in that initial email to update their password and begin filling out some basic information and the Core Beliefs Profile.



    Step 2: Upon password reset, when you log into the system, you will need to fill in some basic demographic information about yourself and your school.





    Step 3: The last step is to respond to questions about your school's mission, staff culture, professional development, instruction, planning, student culture, and school environment. Once you have completed this brief, 5-10 minute survey, you will be able to access our portal, view Culture Match Scores and use our arsenal of convenience features.


    ExpertTip: Your specific answers to each question will be kept private, but will be used to calculate the overall Match score between your school and potential candidates. To help find the right talent and promote retention, be as honest as possible in your responses. Please note that you will *not* be able to view the Culture Match Scores of any potential candidates until the principal completes this profile.



  • Add Content to Your Public Profile

    Completing your public profile on myEDmatch is quick and intuitive, and is the easiest way to market your school and attract potential candidates. Follow the steps below to build a profile that is compelling and that introduces potential candidates to your school.


    ExpertTip: Your Public Profile is indexed by Google and other search engines for both myEDmatch users and the public to see whether you take the time to build it out or not, so we highly recommend spending some time on this profile to put your school's "best foot forward." Taking the time to create a compelling profile now can help attract great candidates, whereas a profile that is less well-managed could turn potential candidates away.


    Step 1: Start by adding your school's logo to your profile. Simply "hover" (put your mouse over) the circle image until you see text that says "Upload Profile Picture."



    You can also upload a background image to the horizontal box at the top of your page. You can upload an image of any size into this box. Smaller images will be placed on a black "chalkboard" background as in the above image. If you don't like the chalkboard border around your image, you can upload an image that is 1280 pixels by 275 pixels. If you don't have software that allows you to do this easily, try using the free photo cropping tool at www.fotor.com.


    Step 2: Fill in written content on your public profile. The information in the green circles will have been automatically filled based upon the principal's answers to the initial school demographic section during sign-up. If these sections are blank, please ask your principal to complete the 5-10 minute profile. You will not be able to see match scores of candidates until the principal completes this brief survey. Once this is completed, in the Public profile, sections we encourage you to complete include: "Our Mission," "Student Achievement Results," "In Our Own Words," "Leadership Opportunities," and "Living Here," in addition to uploading photos or videos to the "slider" sections as described in the next section.


    ExpertTip: School and District profiles are different and have different sections. District managers can toggle between the two using the dropdown menu in the top left of the header. One *any* page in our portal, toggling this to the District will give you the "District overview" view of all of your schools, and allow you to view your district profile, whereas selecting an individual school from this dropdown menu will allow you to "View As" that specific school.



    ExpertTip: As an additional convenience feature, Districts can actually "push out" information from "Our Mission" and "Living Here" sections to *all* schools in their networks with ease by simply clicking "Publish to All Schools." This will push out the information in these sections to all schools in your network.




    Step 3: Complete your profile by adding photos and videos. To any slider section of the profile, you can hit "Upload" or the "+" symbol to upload a photo, video, or pdf/document. For any uploaded document, please make sure that the only periods (.) in the name of the file come before the extension (ie "photo.jpg," "photo.png," "photo.pdf," etc, not "photo.extraperiod.jpg").



    ExpertTip: Schools that have more fully completed profiles garner more attention from prospective applicants. It's important to fill out content for every section of the school profile so that teachers don't see incomplete sections.


    Step 4: If you'd like, you can also invite your current teachers to join myEDmatch so that they can speak to what it's like to work at your school. These teachers will NOT be searchable in teacher search, they will just be visible and linked on your Profile page so potential teacher prospects can talk to them. Be sure to tell your teachers to indicate that they work at your school during the registration process. When your school's teachers join myEDmatch, they will then appear in the dropdown list of staff members you can associate with your school so you can highlight them on your Public Profile.

    ExpertTip: As you change the status of applicants, you can view summaries of where candidates are in the process At-A-Glance from the ribbon at the top of your Dashboard. You can filter by campus and position and see exactly how many candidates are in each stage of your application pipeline.

  • What is a Core Beliefs Profile?

    The Core Beliefs Profile is a simple 5-10 minute survey-like questionnaire designed to determine the level of alignment between your views on educational mission and culture and that of the school administration, and speaks to the likelihood that a teacher will be happy at that school long term. myEDmatch will use the results of your Core Beliefs Profile to suggest schools with similar educational missions, beliefs, and goals as your own.

    The questionnaire includes seven core areas of education:

    • Mission - a shared set of beliefs around the purpose, goals, and aspirations of a school
    • Instruction - a specific approach to teaching and learning used in a school
    • Professional Development - support of teachers' professional growth and advancement
    • Planning - organization around the task of teaching and learning
    • Student Culture - students' shared beliefs, values, and behaviors concerning school
    • School Environment - the management of time, people, and resources through systems and structures

    Within each area is a series of statements, about which you will select the degree to which you agree or disagree. To do so, just click on the radio button indicating your level of agreement for each statement. Don't forget to click Save after completing each core section. It should take you no more than 5-10 minutes to complete. Once you've completed this profile, you can begin searching for jobs that are right for you using our more than 20 filters via the "Jobs" tab!
